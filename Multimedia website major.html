'Intangible' - Synopsis

‘Intangible’ is a short film that follows a teenager named Charlie. He wakes up to the sound of his alarm clock early in the morning, he begins completing homework and as he finishes a gust of wind flies through his bedroom window sending his page flying out onto the street. He leaves his house and begins to chase the page traversing through different settings with different composition styles. He first runs through a street scene where a car drives past sweeping the page further. He next chases the paper through a forest setting where a bird nibs the page in its beak flying through the lush forest which eventually decays as it is being cut down in the background in an excavation site. It is now night time and their silhouettes are traversing through the forest. The bird lands in its nest as the tree is being cut down. A cut occurs and the boy is pictured back in his room writing on the page. It is revealed that the events of the previous scenes were taking place on his page as he is writing a ‘creative’ response. The previous scenes are illustrated on his page as he documents his fictitious ‘adventure’.

‘Intangible’ will combine a variety of production elements including: Stop motion Camera shot footage Motion graphics Rotoscoping and green screen editing Specifically, each scene is to feature a different composition style, displaying product diversity. The short film, ‘Intangible’ will have a duration of around 5 minutes with each scene spanning 30 seconds to 1 minute- the aim is to have 4 main scenes, an introductory title clip and credits. The themes that will be incorporated into this short film are adventure, freedom, achieving your goals and environmental consciousness. All content to be produced will be completed independently complementary to the feedback of my Multimedia teacher.

The Purpose and Process of Creating "Intangible"

The purpose of ‘Intangible’ is to showcase my multimedia skills and abilities that have been acquired throughout the preliminary year. I intend to express various styles within my composition (stop motion, paper motion and animation) to display my comprehension of industrial technology multimedia. ‘Intangible’ is suitable for all people to watch- the genre of ‘Intangible’ is adventure which may better appeal to a teenage / young adult audience forming my intended target audience people aged 15 - 20.

The planning process

Brainstorming and Idea Generation

Storyboarding is crucial in maintaining fluidity from the planning stage up to my final production- an emphasis on maintaining close correlation from my storyboard to my final product is demonstrated in this stage and allows me to remain consistent in my final work

Creation of my set

Set and Prop Development for Scene 1

As part of the plot of my project, the stop motion scene involves the creation of a bedroom, for that reason the set construction of a miniature bedroom has been researched. Within this, further props and an armature are to be created.

Filming and animating

Editing Processes and Shot Collection

A range of images will be taken for scene one, my stop motion scene which will require me to manually combine these pictures to create the perception of a video. The rest of the short film will be created using animation on After Effects and Premiere Pro

Finalising the production

Final Adjustments and Screening

Minor colour & sound corrections will be made to enhance my short film. All editing will be complete & the film will be monitored & reviewed for errors, undergoing an approval process by my multimedia teacher before being transfered to a USB for viewing.
